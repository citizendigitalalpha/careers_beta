Health

Children's nurse

Children's nurses provide care for children and young people with acute or long-term health problems.

Dental nurse

Dental nurses help dentists to care for their patients, and may also carry out reception work and other tasks.

Dental hygienist

Oral health practitioner

Dental hygienists offer advice, information and treatments to prevent and treat tooth decay and gum disease.

Dentist

Dental surgeon, dental practitioner

Dentists diagnose and treat teeth and mouth problems, and work to prevent dental disease and promote oral health.

GP

General practitioner, doctor

General practitioners (GPs) are doctors who provide medical services to people in the community.

Healthcare assistant

Support worker, nursing assistant, nursing auxiliary

Healthcare assistants help look after patients in hospitals or in patientsâ€™ own homes.

Hospital doctor

A hospital doctor diagnoses and treats illness and disease in patients admitted to hospital.

Mental health nurse

Mental health nurses work in hospitals and the community, to support people with a range of mental health issues.

Midwife

Midwives give care and support to pregnant women and their babies, before, during and after childbirth.

Occupational therapist

Occupational therapists help people overcome difficulties caused by physical or mental illness, accidents or ageing.

Pharmacist

Dispensing chemist, community pharmacist, hospital pharmacist

Pharmacists provide expert advice on the use and supply of medicines and medical appliances.

Psychologist

Psychologists study people's behaviour, motivations, thoughts and feelings, to help them overcome or control their problems.

Psychotherapist

Psychotherapists use talking techniques and therapies to help people who are distressed, or have mental health problems.

Physiotherapist

Physiotherapists work with patients to improve their range of movement and promote health and wellbeing.

Radiographer

Medical radiographer

Radiographers use equipment to diagnose or treat patients who are ill or injured.

Play therapist

Play therapists help children to make sense of difficult life experiences or complex psychological issues through play.
