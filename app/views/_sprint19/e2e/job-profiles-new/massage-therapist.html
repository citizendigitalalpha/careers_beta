1. How to become

There are no set requirements but you could get into this job through:

  • a college course in massage therapy
  • an apprenticeship

College

Most massage therapists have a level 3 qualification in beauty therapy or massage.

To do a level 3 qualification, you'll usually need:

  • a level 2 qualification or equivalent experience
  • 4 GCSEs at grade 3 (D) or above, including English and maths or equivalent qualifications

Any course you take should be at least 6 months full time or 12 months part time. If a course is shorter than that, it won't usually prepare you for professional practice.

Contact your local college for details of courses and exact entry requirements.

You may be able to get financial help to do a course.

Apprenticeship

You could also start out in this job by taking an intermediate apprenticeship and moving into an advanced apprenticeship.

To do an apprenticeship, it may help if you have GCSEs in English and maths at grade 3 (D) or above or equivalent qualifications.

See the voluntary register of the Complementary and Natural Healthcare Council (CNHC) for more information, including how to become a member.

2. Skills required

You'll need:

  • manual and practical skills
  • excellent communication and listening skills
  • the ability to put your clients at ease
  • the ability to show empathy to your clients
  • commercial and admin skills to run a business

3. What you'll do

Your work will depend on the type of massage you do and the clients you see. You could offer sports massage, baby and infant massage, or massage therapy for people with medical conditions.

A typical session will include:

  • checking the client's medical history, diet and lifestyle
  • identifying the client's reasons for wanting massage therapy
  • planning a course of treatment
  • applying pressure to areas of the body
  • giving advice to clients about their wellbeing
  • referring clients to medical professionals

4. Career path and progression

With experience you could build up and maintain a reputation and client base, and set up your own business.

You could also train in other complementary therapies like aromatherapy, reiki or reflexology.
