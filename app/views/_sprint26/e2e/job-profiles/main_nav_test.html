How to become a {{profileName}}

There are a number of options and routes which could allow you to become a beauty consultant.

College

You could study for:

  • a level 2 certificate or diploma in beauty consultancy
  • a general qualification in retail, sales or customer service

Requirements:

To get onto a level 2 course you may need:

  • 3 GCSEs at grades 9 to 3 (A* to D)

Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

More information

  • You may be able to get financial help to do a course.
  • Find a web development course near you.

Work your way up

You could get useful experience in any sales role and then apply for a job working for a beauty concession (a commercial operation within a department store).

Apprenticeships

You could do an intermediate or advanced apprenticeship in beauty therapy make-up. These last 12 to 18 months. You may need a level 2 qualification to get onto the advanced apprenticeship. Check with course providers for exact entry requirements.

Requirements:

  • You may need a level 2 qualification to get onto the advanced apprenticeship. Check with course providers for exact entry requirements.
  • Get work experience in sales

Next steps:

  • The Hairdressing and Beauty Industry Authority (HABIA) has more information about careers in beauty consultancy and therapy.
  • Look for a beauty therapist apprenticeship near you.

What it takes

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • a desire to help people
  • customer service skills to put clients at ease
  • excellent speaking and listening skills to understand clients’ needs
  • attention to detail for doing tasks like nail art
  • the ability to work well with your hands for giving treatments like massage
  • reading, writing, spelling and grammar skills for keeping records
  • the ability to sell products and services to earn commission
  • digital skills for communicating and recording information

What you'll do

You could work in a high street salon, beauty clinic, health spa or hotel. You might also work on a cruise ship. You could also be self-employed where you’d work from home, or visit clients in their own homes.

Day to day tasks:

You could be:

  • giving a facial by cleansing, massaging and toning a client’s skin
  • giving a makeover by applying and advising on make-up
  • shaping eyebrows and colouring eyelashes
  • giving a manicure, pedicure or other nail treatment like extensions and nail art
  • removing unwanted facial and body hair
  • giving electro-therapy treatments to improve body tone and shape
  • giving non-surgical skin improvement treatments
  • offering UV (ultraviolet) and spray tanning
  • offering other treatments like massage, aromatherapy, hydrotherapy and reflexology

As well as giving treatments, you may also be:

  • answering the telephone and greeting clients
  • booking appointments
  • checking and ordering supplies
  • keeping notes of clients’ medical histories and treatment programmes
  • referring clients to a doctor if needed

Working Environments:

You could work in a hospital, care home, travel to a client home, GP practice or health center

You might find this work physically demanding, stressful or emotinally draining

You might also need to wear uniform

Career path and progression

You'll usually work 35 to 40 hours a week, Monday to Friday. You may sometimes need to work overtime to meet deadlines.

Most of your work will be office-based at a computer. There may be some out-of-office duties, like attending meetings with clients or partners.

Current opportunities

There are currently no opportunities in this field of work.
