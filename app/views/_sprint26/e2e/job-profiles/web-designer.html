How to become

You'll need a portfolio that demonstrates your creative design skills and shows employers what website work you've done.

You'll need skills in:

  • visual design
  • UX (user experience)
  • SEO (search engine optimisation), marketing and social media
  • coding like HTML and CSS
  • design software like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator
  • content management systems (CMS)

You can develop your skills and build a portfolio of work by:

  • getting paid or voluntary work experience
  • studying at college or university
  • doing an apprenticeship

Work experience

You could start out working as a design assistant or web support assistant and train while you work.

You could also get experience by designing your own websites. You could volunteer for a local community group or charity and create a website for them. Any experience will be helpful in getting a job.

Requirements:

Make sure that you know the latest trends and techniques in web design.

You can do this by:

  • getting involved in free webinars or online tutorials
  • signing up to free online magazines
  • reading the latest blogs for news and tips

College

You could take a college or higher education course in a web design or multimedia subject. Check with local colleges to see what's on offer and what their entry requirements are. Useful subjects include:

  • web design and development
  • multimedia design
  • digital media development
  • interactive computing

Requirements:

To get onto a degree course you'll usually need 5 GCSEs at grade 4 (C) or above and 2 to 3 A levels.

Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

Next steps:

  • UCAS has information on degree courses and entry requirements.
  • You can apply for student finance to cover fees and living costs.

There are also lots of free online tutorials.

Apprenticeships

You could take an advanced level apprenticeship in web design.

Requirements:

You'll usually need at least 3 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) or equivalent, to get a place.

Next steps:

  • Tech Future Careers has more information on tech roles including web design.
  • Find out more about getting into a digital career from the Bubble Digital Career Portal.

Skills required

Skills & Knowledge

You'll need:

  • strong creative skills
  • the ability to pay attention to detail
  • good problem-solving skills and a logical approach to work
  • the ability to explain technical matters clearly
  • an ability to work to deadlines

What you'll do

You could work on any kind of website, from education to shopping. You'll often be responsible for managing the design of your client's other online services like mobile applications, social media accounts and digital marketing campaigns.

Your day-to-day duties might include:

  • meeting clients to discuss what they want their site to do
  • preparing a design plan
  • deciding which branding, text, colours and backgrounds to use
  • laying out pages and positioning buttons, links and pictures using design software
  • adding multimedia features like sound, animation and video
  • testing and improving the design and site
  • uploading the site to a server

You'll work closely with web developers and marketing professionals.

Career path and progression

With experience you could move into design team management or expand your skills to become a web content manager.

You might work towards a move into business management.

You could also work as a freelance web designer, or set up your own web design business.
