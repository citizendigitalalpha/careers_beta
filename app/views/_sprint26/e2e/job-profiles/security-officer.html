How to become

You'll need to complete approved SIA training courses

More information

Registration

  • You'll need a Security Industry Authority (SIA) licence for agency and contractor jobs.

Career tips

Previous experience of working in the police or armed forces could help you get into this job.

An employer will carry out checks on your personal and work history (up to 10 years). You need to let them know about any criminal convictions before you apply for a licence.

You won't need an SIA licence for in-house security jobs at your employer's premises.

Further information

Skills for Security has more information about becoming a security officer.

What it takes

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • patience and the ability to remain calm in stressful situations
  • the ability to work well with others
  • problem-solving skills for dealing with emergencies
  • concentration skills to stay alert and aware of your surroundings
  • customer service skills for challenging people in a polite but firm manner
  • excellent speaking and listening skills for negotiating
  • reading, writing, spelling and grammar skills for following instructions and writing reports
  • digital skills for communicating and recording information

Restrictions

You'll need

  • to pass identity and enhanced criminal records checks

    An employer will carry out checks on your personal and work history (up to 10 years). You need to let them know about any criminal convictions before you apply for a licence.

  • a Security Industry Authority (SIA) licence for agency and contractor jobs.

    You won't need an SIA licence for in-house security jobs at your employer's premises.

  • If you work as a contractor on a building site, you'll also need a Construction Skills Certification Scheme card (CSCS).

  • To be aged 18 or over

What you'll do

You could work in shops, offices, schools, sports and entertainment venues, airports and docks. Your role will depend on the organisation you work for.

Day to day tasks

You could be:

  • patrolling, securing and monitoring premises and grounds
  • searching aircraft, passengers and luggage, and guarding the perimeter of airports
  • supervising on the door for a large organisation

Working environment

In this job you may:

  • work in shops, offices, schools, sports and entertainment venues, airports and docks
  • need a driving licence
  • find the work physically demanding
  • face danger
  • wear a uniform

Career path and progression

With experience, you could progress to senior or chief security officer, then supervisor.

With further qualifications you could go into security management or training, or set up your own security company.
