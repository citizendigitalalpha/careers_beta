How to become

There are a number of ways to become a catering mananger, you could:

Work your way up

You could work your way up to a management position by getting a job in a role like a catering assistant. With experience and training, you could take on more responsibility and supervise less experienced colleagues. You could then apply for an assistant manager post and train while you're working.

Apprenticeships

You could take a higher apprenticeship as a hospitality manager if you've got work experience in catering and have also worked as a supervisor.

Requirements:

To take a higher apprenticeship, you will need work experience in catering and have also worked as a supervisor.

If you don't have any supervisory experience, you could take an advanced apprenticeship as a hospitality supervisor.

Next steps:

  • Look for a hospitality apprenticeship near you

Collage

You could do a degree. Useful subjects include:

  • business and hospitality management
  • culinary arts or culinary management
  • hotel and catering management

Some large contract catering companies and hotel chains run graduate training schemes. Entry requirements vary.

Requirements:

To get onto a course, you'll usually need at least:

  • GCSEs in English and maths at grades 4 (C) or equivalent
  • 96 UCAS points or 2 A level passes

Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

Next steps:

  • UCAS has information on degree courses and entry requirements.
  • You can apply for student finance to cover fees and living costs.

Skills required

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • excellent communication and negotiation skills
  • the ability to motivate and manage staff
  • budgeting skills
  • the ability to keep calm under pressure

Restrictions

You'll need:

  • a background check if you're working around children or vulnerable adults

What you'll do

You may work in an organisation like a school, hospital, hotel or pub, or prepare food to be delivered to food outlets, event sites and homes.

Your day-to-day tasks may include:

  • planning and developing menus to increase sales
  • recruiting and training staff
  • organising shifts and rotas
  • financial planning, managing budgets and stock control
  • meeting suppliers and negotiating contracts
  • monitoring the quality of the service to customers
  • running the business in line with health and safety and food hygiene regulations
  • catering for special diets and meeting nutritional needs

During food service, you'll supervise the kitchen and waiting staff, ensuring that the food goes out on time and is of a high standard.

Career path and progression

With experience you could move into larger organisations or those offering more specialised work like event catering.

You could also study for further qualifications in hospitality.

Another option is to start your own contract catering business.
