Medical secretary

Medical secretaries provide office support in hospitals, GP surgeries and universities.

Salary: £16,000 to £28,000 average per year
Hours: 37 to 40 hours per week

1. Entry requirements

You'll need GCSEs (A* to C) in maths and English. A typing or word processing qualification may help you get a job.

You'll also need office experience. A knowledge of medical terms could help too.

You could get into this job through an apprenticeship.

2. Skills required

You'll need:

  • administration and organisational skills
  • accuracy and attention to detail
  • excellent grammar and spelling

3. What you'll do

You could work for a hospital doctor, NHS manager, hospital consultant or a GP. You could work in the NHS or in a private company.

Your day-to-day duties may include:

  • handling questions from patients
  • organising a doctor's diary and booking travel
  • managing a waiting list of patients
  • updating patient records
  • sending samples for medical testing
  • typing letters, clinical reports and filing
  • monitoring an office budget
  • dealing with confidential information

4. Salary

Starter: £16,500 to £19,500

Experienced: £19,000 to £22,500 (senior medical secretary)

Highly Experienced: £21,500 to £28,500 (lead medical secretary)

These figures are a guide.

5. Working hours, patterns and environment

You'll usually work in an office, 9am to 5pm, Monday to Friday.

6. Career path and progression

In a large organisation, you could manage a team of secretaries.

With further training, you could become an administration manager, office manager or GP practice manager.
