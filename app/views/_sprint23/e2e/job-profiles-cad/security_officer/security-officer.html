How to become

You'll need a portfolio that demonstrates your creative design skills and shows employers what website work you've done.

You'll need skills in:

  • visual design
  • UX (user experience)
  • SEO (search engine optimisation), marketing and social media
  • coding like HTML and CSS
  • design software like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator
  • content management systems (CMS)

You can develop your skills and build a portfolio of work by:

  • getting paid or voluntary work experience
  • studying at college or university
  • doing an apprenticeship

  • signing up to free online magazines
  • reading the latest blogs for news and tips

You could take a college or higher education course in a web design or multimedia subject. Check with local colleges to see what's on offer and what their entry requirements are. Useful subjects include:

  • web design and development
  • multimedia design
  • digital media development
  • interactive computing

Requirements:

To get onto a degree course you'll usually need 5 GCSEs at grade 4 (C) or above and 2 to 3 A levels.

Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

Next steps:

You could also work as a freelance web designer, or set up your own web design business.
