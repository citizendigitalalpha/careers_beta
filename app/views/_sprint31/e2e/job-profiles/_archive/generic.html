How to become

You could develop the skills you need for this role by:

  • studying at university or college
  • doing an apprenticeship
  • getting paid or voluntary work experience

University

You could do a foundation degree, Higher National Diploma (HND) or degree. Relevant subjects include:

  • web design and development
  • computer science
  • digital media development
  • software engineering

You'll study for 3 to 4 years, do a year's practical work experience, then study for another 2 years. You'll follow this with further practical training, an exam, and registration with the ARB.

Requirements

You'll usually need:

  • 2 to 3 A levels for a degree

More information

  • UCAS has information on degree courses and entry requirements.
  • You can apply for student finance to cover fees and living costs.

College

You could do a level 2, 3 or 4 course in web development. These can last from a few months to a year.

Check with colleges and universities for exact entry requirements.

Requirements

You'll usually need:

  • 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) including English and maths

More information

  • You may be able to get financial help to do a course.
  • Find a web development course near you.

Apprenticeship

There are 3 levels of apprenticeship you could do in IT, software, web and telecoms professionals. These are intermediate, advanced and higher apprenticeship. These could take up to 2 and a half years to complete.

Alternatively, if you have A levels, you could go straight into a higher apprenticeship lasting 18 months.

Requirements

You'll usually need:

  • 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) including English and maths
  • 2 to 3 A levels for a higher apprenticeship

More information:

  • You may be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

Work

You could get a job in IT at any level and then work your way up. You could learn on the job, or take part-time qualifications online or at college.

Volunteering

You could also get the skills you need by volunteering to set up a website for a charity or an event.

What it takes

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • excellent knowledge of web technologies and database applications
  • programming skills
  • knowledge of web design, usability and interactivity
  • creative skills to turn clients' ideas into workable plans
  • a logical approach and problem-solving skills
  • knowledge of agile project management tools and techniques
  • communication and teamworking skills

You may need project management skills for more senior roles or freelance work.

Restrictions and Requirements

You'll need to:

  • be aged between 18 and 28 years and 11 months
  • meet the British army nationality and residency rules
  • get a GP’s medical report and pass a full army medical

You'll also usually need:

  • 7 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C), or equivalent, including English, maths and a science or foreign language
  • 2 A levels (or equivalent) with 72 UCAS Tariff points
  • You’ll also need clearance from the Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) and a security check.

What you'll do

Depending on your qualifications, you'll service and repair heating, water and sanitation systems in domestic and commercial premises.

Day-to-day tasks

On a daily basis you could be:

  • measuring and planning to give cost and time estimates
  • cutting, bending and joining pipes and fittings
  • installing water, drainage and heating systems
  • finding and fixing faults
  • servicing gas and oil-fired central heating systems and radiators
  • installing and fixing domestic appliances like showers and washing machines
  • dealing with emergency call-outs like boiler breakdowns or blocked drains
  • fitting weather-proof materials, joints and flashings to roofs, chimneys and walls

Working environment

You'll be working in all weathers. Some of your work will be in confined spaces. You could also work on scaffolding or from ladders to repair roof guttering or leadwork.

You'll usually wear protective clothing, like overalls and safety shoes.

You'll travel locally between jobs. You may have to work away from home on some contracts. A driving licence will usually be required.

Career path and progression

With experience, you could specialise in a particular area, like e-commerce or move up to a more senior role like lead programmer or project leader.

You could also move into other IT fields, like systems analysis or IT project management.
