1. How to become

You could:

  • study at college for a Level 3 Certificate or Diploma in Travel and Tourism
  • do an advanced apprenticeship in travel consultancy
  • start as a trainee travel agent and receive on-the-job training from your employer

College

To do a Level 3 Diploma in Travel and Tourism you'll usually need GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C), which must include English language, or equivalent qualifications.

You may be able to get financial help to study.

Find a travel and tourism course near you.

Apprenticeship

Many travel agencies offer advanced apprenticeships where you can work towards the Level 3 Certificate or Diploma in Travel and Tourism.

You'll usually need GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C), including English language and maths. Employers may also accept equivalent level 2 qualifications.

Work experience

You could start as a trainee travel agent and receive on-the-job training from your employer. While working, you could study one of these qualifications:

  • Level 2 or 3 Certificate in Travel and Tourism
  • Level 3 (Extended) Certificate/Diploma in Travel and Tourism
  • Level 4 Certificate/Diploma in Management for Travel and Tourism
  • Level 5 Certificate/Diploma in Management for Travel and Tourism

It may help if you have customer service experience and can speak a foreign language.

The Institute of Travel & Tourism (ITT) and Careers that Move have more information about the industry.

2. Skills required

You'll need:

  • excellent customer service skills and telephone manner
  • strong communication and sales skills
  • the ability to prioritise and cope with pressure at busy times

3. What you'll do

You may work in a high street travel agents or a call centre, specialising in personal or business travel.

Depending on your role, your day-to-day tasks may include:

  • understanding and meeting customers' needs
  • using your geographical knowledge to help customers find a suitable package holiday or plan independent travel
  • making bookings and payments using online computer systems
  • advising customers about passports, insurance, visas, vaccinations, tours and vehicle hire
  • informing customers of changes like cancelled flights
  • arranging refunds and handling complaints
  • meeting sales targets
  • keeping up to date with developments in the travel industry

4. Career path and progression

With training and experience, there may be opportunities to progress into senior roles like branch or call centre manager, operations director, regional director or managing director.

You could also move into other roles in the travel sector, like tour operating.
