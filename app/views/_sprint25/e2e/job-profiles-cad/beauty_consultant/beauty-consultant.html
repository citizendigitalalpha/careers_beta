1. How to become a {{profileName}}

There are no set requirements, but it could help if you were to:

  • do a college qualification
  • get work experience in sales
  • do an apprenticeship

College

You could study for:

  • a level 2 certificate or diploma in beauty consultancy
  • a general qualification in retail, sales or customer service

To get onto a level 2 course you may need 3 GCSEs at grades 9 to 3 (A* to D). Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

You may be able to get financial help.

Work

You could get useful experience in any sales role and then apply for a job working for a beauty concession (a commercial operation within a department store).

Apprenticeship

You could do an intermediate or advanced apprenticeship in beauty therapy make-up. These last 12 to 18 months. You may need a level 2 qualification to get onto the advanced apprenticeship. Check with course providers for exact entry requirements

The Hairdressing and Beauty Industry Authority (HABIA) has more information about careers in beauty consultancy and therapy

What it takes

You might need:

Technology Skills

  • word processing software - Microsoft Word

Knowledge

  • customer and personal service — assessing customer needs, meeting quality standards for services, and checking customer satisfaction
  • English language — knowledge of spelling and grammar, including the rules of putting words and sentences together
  • sales and marketing — knowledge of the principles and methods for showing, promoting, and selling products or services

Skills

  • active listening — giving full attention to what other people are saying
  • speaking — talking to others to give information effectively
  • helping people — actively looking for ways to help people

Abilities

  • near vision — the ability to see clearly at close range (within a few feet of the viewer)
  • arm-hand steadiness — the ability to keep your hand and arm steady while moving your arm or while holding your arm and hand in one position

What you'll do

Your specific day-to-day duties could include:

  • sterilising equipment and cleaning work areas
  • examining clients' skin to assess their skin condition and appearance
  • cleansing clients' skin with water, creams, or lotions
  • demonstrating how to clean and care for skin properly and recommending skin-care routines
  • selecting and applying cosmetic products, like creams, lotions, and tonics
  • performing simple extractions to remove blackheads
  • keeping up to date with the latest industry trends, products, research, and treatments
  • deciding which products or colours will improve a clients' skin quality and appearance
  • treating the skin to improve its appearance using techniques and products like peels and masks
  • referring clients to a doctor for the treatment of serious skin problems
