1. How to become a {{ profileName }}

Each school sets its own entry requirements. Check job vacancies with your local education authority to find out what each school wants.

You could do:

  • a college course
  • an apprenticeship
  • training on the job

You'll usually need:

  • experience of working with children
  • GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) in English and maths, or equivalent qualifications
  • to pass the enhanced background checks your employer or college arrange for you

There is strong competition for jobs, so a qualification in nursery work, childcare, playwork or youth work can give you an advantage.

College

You could study for qualifications like a level 2 or 3 certificate in support for learning and teaching in schools.

Find a course at a college near you.

You may be able to get financial help to do a course.

If you're under 19, these courses are free.

Apprenticeship

You could also get in through an intermediate or advanced apprenticeship in childcare or supporting teaching and learning in schools.

Work

If you don't have any qualifications or experience, you may get into the job if you show you can work well with children, teachers and parents. Many teaching assistants volunteer before getting paid work.
