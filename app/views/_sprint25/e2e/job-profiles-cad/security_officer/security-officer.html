1. How to become a {{profileName}}

You'll need a Security Industry Authority (SIA) licence for agency and contractor jobs. To get this, you must:

  • be aged 18 or over
  • pass identity and enhanced criminal records checks
  • complete approved SIA training

An employer will carry out checks on your personal and work history (up to 10 years). You need to let them know about any criminal convictions before you apply for a licence

You won't need an SIA licence for in-house security jobs at your employer's premises. If you work as a contractor on a building site, you'll also need a Construction Skills Certification Scheme card (CSCS)

Previous experience of working in the police or armed forces could help you get into this job.

Skills for Security has more information about becoming a security officer.

What you'll do

Your specific day-to-day duties could include:

  • locking the doors and gates of entrances and exits to secure buildings
  • answering alarms and investigating disturbances
  • monitoring and authorising the arrival and departure of employees and visitors,to guard against theft and maintain the security of premises
  • writing reports on equipment or property damage, theft, the presence of unauthorised people, or unusual activity
  • patrolling industrial or commercial premises to detect signs of intrusion and make sure doors, windows, and gates are secure
  • calling the police or fire services in the event of emergencies
  • responding to medical emergencies by giving basic first aid or by getting help from paramedics
  • circulating among visitors and employees to keep order and protect property
  • warning people about breaking laws and evicting people from the premises where necessary
  • answering telephone calls to take messages, answer questions, and give information during non-business hours
