How to become a {{profileName}}

You'll need a Security Industry Authority (SIA) licence for agency and contractor jobs. To get this, you must:

  • be aged 18 or over
  • pass identity and enhanced criminal records checks
  • complete approved SIA training

An employer will carry out checks on your personal and work history (up to 10 years). You need to let them know about any criminal convictions before you apply for a licence

You won't need an SIA licence for in-house security jobs at your employer's premises. If you work as a contractor on a building site, you'll also need a Construction Skills Certification Scheme card (CSCS)

Previous experience of working in the police or armed forces could help you get into this job.

Skills for Security has more information about becoming a security officer.

What it takes

You'll need:

  • problem-solving skills
  • concentration skills
  • customer service skills
  • speaking and listening skills
  • the ability to use English - reading, writing, spelling and grammar skills

What you'll do

Your role will vary depending on the industry you work in, and could include:

  • patrolling, securing and monitoring premises
  • guarding cash or other valuables in a security vehicle
  • airport security including searching aircraft, passengers and luggage, and guarding the airport boundaries
  • supervising on the door for a large organisation

Your working environment

For some jobs you'll need a driving licence. If you’re moving or protecting cash or valuables, you’ll spend a lot of your time in a security van.

In some roles you’ll work mainly outside. You may spend long periods of time standing, ng or sitting.

You’ll usually wear a uniform and protective clothing, like a helmet and a high-visibility jacket.

Career path and progression

With experience, you could progress to senior or chief security officer, then supervisor.

With further qualifications you could go into security management or training, or set up your own security company.

Current opportunities
