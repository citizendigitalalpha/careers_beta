1. How to become

You could:

  • do an apprenticeship
  • do a degree
  • teach yourself

Whichever way you go, you'll usually need 4 or 5 GCSEs including English and maths at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) or equivalent qualifications, plus some programming ability or a strong interest in the subject.

Apprenticeship

You could do an advanced apprenticeship as a software development technician.

It will usually take 12 to 18 months.

You can then move onto a higher apprenticeship as a software developer. This can take between 1 and 4 years.

College or university

You could do a foundation degree, higher national diploma (HND) or a degree in a subject like:

  • computer science
  • information technology
  • software development
  • software engineering
  • maths
  • business information systems

UCAS has information on degree courses and entry requirements.

You can apply for student finance to cover fees and living costs for higher education.

Full-time courses at university usually take 3 years. College and HND courses take 2 years full time.

Find a college course near you.

You may be able to get financial help to study at college.

Home study

Many software developers teach themselves and there are many free online learning resources available.

Particularly if you have taught yourself, companies will look to see if you are someone who understands programming languages and frameworks, project management and software development methods.

The Chartered Institute for IT (BCS) has more information about training and qualifications.

What it takes

You might need:

Technology Skills

  • configuration management software
  • database management system software
  • database user interface and query software
  • development environment software

Knowledge

  • computers and electronics — knowledge of circuit boards, processors, chips, electronic equipment, and computer hardware and software
  • engineering and technology — knowledge of engineering and technology to apply principles, techniques, procedures, and equipment to the design and production of goods and services
  • English language — knowledge of spelling and grammar, including the rules of putting words and sentences together
  • mathematics — knowledge of arithmetic, algebra, geometry, calculus, statistics, and their applications

Skills

  • programming — writing computer programs
  • systems analysis — deciding how a system should work and looking at how changes in conditions, operations, and the environment may affect it
  • systems evaluation — identifying ways to measure system performance and the actions needed to improve or correct performance
  • decision making — looking at the risks and benefits of possible actions and choosing the most appropriate one

Abilities

  • written comprehension — the ability to read and understand information and ideas presented in writing
  • information ordering — the ability to arrange things or actions in a certain order or pattern according to a specific rule or set of rules (this could be patterns of numbers, letters, words or pictures)

What you'll do

Your specific day-to-day duties could include:

  • adjusting existing software to correct errors, allow it to adapt to new hardware, or to improve its performance
  • analysing user needs and software requirements to decide on a design which will keep to time and budget
  • working with systems analysts, engineers and programmers to design systems and get information on project limitations and capabilities, performance requirements and interfaces
  • storing, retrieving, and using data to analyse system capabilities and requirements
  • designing, developing and modifying software systems, using scientific analysis and mathematical models
  • developing and directing software system testing, programming, and documentation
  • supervising the work of programmers, technologists and technicians
  • setting system performance standards
  • managing system installations and monitoring equipment to ensure quality
  • consulting with customers about system design and maintenance

Career path and progression

With experience, you could become a senior developer, with team or project management, planning or research responsibilities. You could move into related areas like systems design, IT architecture and business systems analysis.

You could also set up your own business or work as a consultant.
