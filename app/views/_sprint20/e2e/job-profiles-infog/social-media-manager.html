1. How to become

You could:

  • do a degree at university
  • get work experience and train on the job
  • do an apprenticeship

University

Useful subjects include:

  • marketing and public relations
  • digital and social media marketing
  • internet design
  • digital media and communication
  • business management

To do a degree you'll usually need at least:

  • 5 GCSEs at grades 4 (C) or above, including English language
  • 2 A levels (any subjects)

Check with course providers for exact entry requirements. You might be able to use equivalent level qualifications to get onto a course.

UCAS has information on degree courses and entry requirements.

You can apply for student finance to cover fees and living costs.

Work experience

You'll need to show that you understand and use social media. You can do this by:

  • managing your own social media profiles
  • setting up and writing your own blog, and tracking use and popularity
  • volunteering to manage a charitable organisation's social media channels
  • getting involved in social media for the company you already work for

You'll need IT skills like:

  • knowledge of search engine optimisation (SEO) methods
  • experience of graphic design and digital editing software for adding videos, images and infographics

You'll keep up to date by joining webinars about social media trends.

Apprenticeship

You could start out by taking an advanced apprenticeship as a digital marketer. To get a place, you'll usually need at least 5 GCSEs at grades 4 (C) including English language and maths.

You can find information on training from the The Institute of Direct and Digital Marketing and the CAM Foundation.

You can find out more about marketing careers through the Chartered Institute of Marketing.

2. Skills required

You'll need:

  • an eye for detail and the ability to work accurately
  • the ability to deal with more than one task at a time
  • creativity
  • presentation skills
  • an analytical approach to data
  • writing skills

3. What you'll do

You'll monitor and upload content to sites like:

  • Facebook and Twitter
  • Instagram and Pinterest
  • YouTube and Vine

Your duties may include:

  • updating social media sites
  • writing blogs, articles and posts
  • responding to social media posts and developing discussions
  • checking online for company mentions and customer feedback
  • searching for interesting posts, news and articles to attract site visitors
  • overseeing competitions and campaigns promoting your company
  • taking part in conferences and group chat relevant to your industry or company
  • educating other staff on social media use
  • promoting social media use within your company
  • developing strategies to increase your audience
  • using social media tools like Hootsuite, TweetDeck or Buffer to manage multiple sites
  • using web tracking tools like Google Analytics, Social Report or Bitly
